At the base of Mount Fuji, there's a forest so dense and silent that compasses don't work. Welcome to Aokigahara — Japan's Sea of Trees. Aokigahara sits on volcanic rock from Mount Fuji's last eruption in 1707. The ground is porous, absorbing sound completely. Stand still and you hear nothing. No birds. No wind. Just silence. The forest is famous for its ice caves — natural formations where ice remains year-round, even in summer. And for its lava tubes, tunnels carved by molten rock centuries ago. Despite its reputation, Aokigahara is ecologically unique. The trees grow from moss-covered volcanic rock in twisted, surreal shapes. It's one of the most photographed forests in Japan for its otherworldly beauty. The forest has marked hiking trails and is actually a popular destination for nature lovers.
