# Hidden Japan: Aokigahara — The Forest at the Foot of Fuji

**[VISUAL: Aerial view of dense green forest at base of Mount Fuji, mist rising]**
**VO:** At the base of Mount Fuji, there's a forest so dense and silent that compasses don't work. Welcome to Aokigahara — Japan's Sea of Trees.

**[VISUAL: Forest floor covered in volcanic rock, twisted roots]**
**VO:** Aokigahara sits on volcanic rock from Mount Fuji's last eruption in 1707. The ground is porous, absorbing sound completely. Stand still and you hear nothing. No birds. No wind. Just silence.

**[VISUAL: Ice caves and lava tubes beneath the forest]**
**VO:** The forest is famous for its ice caves — natural formations where ice remains year-round, even in summer. And for its lava tubes, tunnels carved by molten rock centuries ago.

**[VISUAL: Sunlight filtering through dense canopy]**
**VO:** Despite its reputation, Aokigahara is ecologically unique. The trees grow from moss-covered volcanic rock in twisted, surreal shapes. It's one of the most photographed forests in Japan for its otherworldly beauty.

**[VISUAL: Hiking trail with colored markers]**
**VO:** The forest has marked hiking trails and is actually a popular destination for nature lovers. The Nakayama Nanahyakume trail offers breathtaking views of Mount Fuji.
