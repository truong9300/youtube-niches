If you were born after 1964, your preservation age is 60. Once you turn preservation age AND retire, you can access your super.
You can take it as a lump sum, set up an income stream, or move it into an account-based pension — which is tax-free once you're over 60.
That's right. In Australia, once you turn 60, all super withdrawals are completely tax-free. It's one of the most generous retirement systems in the world.
Before I wrap up, here are the most common mistakes Australians make:
One: Having multiple super accounts. Every time you change jobs, you might open a new account. Each one charges fees. Over a lifetime, that could cost you $70,000. Use the ATO's online tool to find and consolidate your lost super.
Two: Not checking your insurance. Most super funds automatically include life insurance and income protection. If you're young with no dependents, you might be paying for cover you don't need.
Three: Sticking with the default "MySuper" balanced option. If you're under 40, a high-growth option could earn you significantly more over the long term.
Superannuation is the single most powerful wealth-building tool available to Australians. But only if you understand it and actively manage it.
