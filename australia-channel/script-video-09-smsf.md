# Self-Managed Super Fund (SMSF): Is It Worth It?

**Độ dài:** ~8 phút | **Series:** Superannuation Explained (Video #9)

**[VISUAL: Person at desk managing investments like a professional trader, charts everywhere]**
**VO:** Imagine having total control over your super. You choose exactly which shares to buy, which properties to invest in, and when to sell. That's the promise of a Self-Managed Super Fund — an SMSF.

**[VISUAL: SMSF stats: 600K funds, $1T assets, average balance $1.5M]**
**VO:** There are over 600,000 SMSFs in Australia, controlling over $1 trillion in assets. The average balance is $1.5 million. But here's the truth most people don't tell you.

**[VISUAL: Rules - max 6 members, all trustees, cannot be employee of fund]**
**VO:** An SMSF means you're the trustee. You're legally responsible for investment decisions, compliance, tax returns, and audits. Break the rules and the ATO can impose harsh penalties.

**[VISUAL: Cost breakdown - setup $3K, annual accounting $2-3K, audit $500-1K, ATO levy $259]**
**VO:** The cost. Setting up an SMSF costs around $3,000. Annual accounting and auditing runs $2,500 to $4,000. Plus the ATO levy of $259. That's $3,000 a year before you invest a dollar.

**[VISUAL: Break-even chart: $200K SMSF costs same as retail fund, under $200K retail is cheaper]**
**VO:** Most experts say you need at least $200,000 in super for an SMSF to be cost-effective. Below that, the fixed costs eat all the benefits.

**[VISUAL: Pros: control, direct property, crypto, overseas shares, estate planning]**
**VO:** The upside: you can invest in direct property, cryptocurrency, overseas shares, and even artwork. You can borrow within your SMSF for property. Estate planning is more flexible.

**[VISUAL: Cons: admin burden, compliance risk, time commitment, personal liability]**
**VO:** The downside: massive admin burden, you need to understand complex super laws, personal liability for mistakes, and managing it takes serious time.

**[VISUAL: Decision flowchart: $200K+ and want control? → SMSF. Under $200K? → Industry fund]**
**VO:** The verdict: if you have over $200K in super, understand investing, and want direct control of assets like property — an SMSF can work. Otherwise, a good Industry fund with a high-growth option outperforms SMSFs for most people.
