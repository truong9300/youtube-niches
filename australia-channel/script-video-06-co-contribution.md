### 00:30 – What Is the Super Co-Contribution? (1:30)

**[VISUAL: Government building + mũi tên tiền → super account]**

**VO:** The Super Co-Contribution is a government program where the ATO deposits money directly into your super account if you make after-tax contributions.

**[VISUAL: Công thức — you put in $1 → government adds $0.50]**

**VO:** For every dollar you contribute from your after-tax income, the government will add up to 50 cents. The maximum co-contribution is $500 per year.

**[VISUAL: $1,000 after-tax → $500 từ chính phủ → $1,500 total]**

**VO:** So if you put in $1,000 of your own money after tax, the government adds $500. Total: $1,500 going into your super. A free 50% return instantly.

**[VISUAL: Không phải tax deduction, không phải rebate — tiền mặt thật]**

**VO:** This is not a tax deduction. You don't need to owe tax. You just need to have earned income and put after-tax money into super.

---

### 02:00 – Who Is Eligible? (2:00)

**[VISUAL: 3 tiêu chí chính]**

**VO:** So who qualifies? Let's break it down.

**[VISUAL: Tiêu chí 1 — Income under $58,445]**

**VO:** Criterion one: Your total income must be under $58,445 per year. The full $500 is available if you earn under $45,000. Between $45,000 and $58,445, it phases out gradually.

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ phase-out — $45K → $500, $58K → $0]**

**VO:** For every dollar you earn over $45,000, the co-contribution reduces by 3.33 cents. At $58,445, it reaches zero.

**[VISUAL: Tiêu chí 2 — You must make after-tax contributions]**

**VO:** Criterion two: You must make after-tax (non-concessional) contributions to your super. This is money you've already paid tax on. You can't claim the co-contribution on salary sacrifice or employer contributions.

**[VISUAL: Tiêu chí 3 — Under 71 and meet the work test]**

**VO:** Criterion three: You must be under 71 years old and meet the work test, which means you're employed and earning at least 10% of your income from employment or business.

**[VISUAL: Total super balance under $1.9M]**

**VO:** And one more: your total super balance must be under $1.9 million. But let's be honest — if your super is over $1.9 million, you probably don't need the $500.

### 06:00 – How to Make the Contribution (1:30)

**[VISUAL: Step-by-step — BPAY or direct transfer]**

**VO:** How do you actually do it? Two ways.

**[VISUAL: Option 1 — BPAY through your super fund]**

**VO:** Option one: Log into your super fund's online portal. Most have a "Make a contribution" button. Enter your bank details, choose after-tax contribution, and send the money. It takes 2 minutes.

**[VISUAL: Option 2 — Direct transfer from bank]**

**VO:** Option two: Use BPAY. Your super fund has a BPAY biller code and reference number on their website or app. Send money directly from your bank.

**[VISUAL: Chú ý — phải ghi đúng loại contribution]**

**VO:** The key thing: make sure you select "non-concessional" or "after-tax" contribution. If your super fund gets it wrong and codes it as before-tax, the ATO won't know you made an eligible contribution.

**[VISUAL: ATO tự động tính — không cần claim]**

**VO:** Here's the best part: you don't need to apply for the co-contribution. The ATO automatically calculates it when you lodge your tax return. If eligible, the money appears in your super account within 60 days of you filing.

### 07:30 – The Spouse Contribution Tax Offset (1:00)

**[VISUAL: Cặp đôi — "Spouse Contribution"]**

**VO:** And there's a related benefit if you're married or in a de facto relationship.

**[VISUAL: Tính toán — contribute $3,000 to low-income spouse → $540 tax offset]**

**VO:** If you contribute after-tax money to your spouse's super and they earn under $40,000, you can claim a tax offset of up to $540.

**[VISUAL: Điều kiện — both eligible]**

**VO:** Your spouse's super contribution gets the co-contribution too. So a low-income couple could receive up to $1,000 in co-contributions plus $540 in tax offsets. That's $1,540 in government benefits from $4,000 in contributions.

### 08:30 – Common Mistakes (1:00)

**[VISUAL: Mistake 1 — contributing before-tax by accident]**

**VO:** Mistake one: accidentally making before-tax contributions. Check your fund's payment instructions carefully. Use "non-concessional" or "after-tax" wording.

**[VISUAL: Mistake 2 — missing the cutoff]**

**VO:** Mistake two: missing the deadline. Contributions must be received by your super fund before June 30 to count for that financial year. Don't leave it to the last week.

**[VISUAL: Mistake 3 — earning too much]**

**VO:** Mistake three: not checking your income. If your income is close to $58,445, even a small bonus or pay rise could reduce your co-contribution. Plan accordingly.

**[VISUAL: Mistake 4 — ignoring it entirely]**

**VO:** And the biggest mistake — doing nothing. The co-contribution is free money. If you earn under $58K and can spare even $20 a week, you're leaving free cash on the table.
