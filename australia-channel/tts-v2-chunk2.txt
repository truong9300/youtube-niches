But here's the thing: what if you salary sacrifice just 1-2% of your salary? That's barely noticeable in your pay, but transformative in retirement.
Myth three: "My employer won't let me." Actually, by law, all employers MUST offer salary sacrifice to all employees if requested. It's not optional for them.
One real caution: if you receive Centrelink benefits or have a low income, salary sacrifice might reduce your eligibility. Talk to a financial advisor or check with Centrelink first.
And here's a game-changer for young Australians — the First Home Super Saver Scheme.
Under the FHSSS, you can voluntarily contribute up to $15,000 per year into your super — up to $50,000 total — and withdraw it for your first home deposit.
The tax advantage means you save 17% more compared to saving outside super. For a $50,000 deposit, that's an extra $8,500 — just from tax efficiency.
Salary sacrifice into super → FHSSS → first home. It's one of the most underutilised pathways to home ownership in Australia.
