What if I told you there's a completely legal way to pay less tax, grow your retirement savings faster, and have the government actually encourage you to do it?
It's called salary sacrifice. And according to the ATO, less than 15% of Australians use it. That means 85% of workers are leaving thousands of dollars on the table every single year.
In this video, I'll explain exactly how salary sacrifice works, why it's so powerful, and how you can set it up in under 10 minutes.

Over 25 years, at 7% returns, that single year's salary sacrifice grows to over $47,000. If Sarah does this every year for 25 years, she'd have over $680,000 extra in her super compared to not doing it.
$680,000. From $200 a week. That's the power of salary sacrifice.
But you can't salary sacrifice unlimited amounts. The government sets annual caps.
In 2026, the concessional contributions cap — which includes your employer's 11.5% super guarantee plus any salary sacrifice — is $30,000 per year.
So if Sarah's employer already puts in $10,925, she can salary sacrifice up to $19,075 more before hitting the cap.
Go over the cap, and you pay excess contributions tax at your marginal rate plus an interest charge. So know your numbers.
But here's a secret: if you didn't use your full cap in previous years, you can carry forward unused amounts for up to 5 years. That means someone with a total super balance under $500,000 could potentially contribute up to $150,000 in a single year.
So how do you actually set this up? It's surprisingly simple.
Step one: Write to your employer's payroll department. Just a simple email: "I'd like to salary sacrifice X dollars per pay period into my super fund."
Step two: Choose your amount. Most people start with $100-$200 per week. Remember, you're reducing your take-home pay — make sure you can afford it.
Step three: Done. Your employer handles everything. The money goes into your super before tax is calculated. You don't have to do anything else.
Pro tip: check your super account after 2-4 weeks to make sure the contributions are showing up. Mistakes happen.
Myth one: "I can't afford to sacrifice money." Reality: if you earn over $45,000, sacrificing $50 a week only reduces your take-home pay by $34. The rest is tax you would have paid anyway.
You don't feel $34 a week. But that $50 growing at 7% for 30 years becomes $5,200. Per week.
Myth two: "I need the money now." Fair point — super is locked until preservation age.
