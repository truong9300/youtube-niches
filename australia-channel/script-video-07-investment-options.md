# Investment Options Inside Super: Growth vs Balanced vs Conservative

**Độ dài:** ~8 phút | **Series:** Superannuation Explained (Video #7)

**[VISUAL: Three paths diverging - aggressive green up arrow, steady blue middle, flat grey conservative]**
**VO:** You have $100,000 in super. Without you doing anything, your money is invested somewhere. The question is: where? And the answer could mean the difference between retiring with $500,000 or $1,000,000.

**[VISUAL: Pie charts showing Growth, Balanced, Conservative allocations]**
**VO:** In this video, I'll explain the three main investment options inside Australian super funds — Growth, Balanced, and Conservative — and which one you should pick based on your age.

**[VISUAL: MySuper balanced default icon]**
**VO:** Most Australians are in the default "MySuper Balanced" option. Balanced means roughly 70% in growth assets like shares and property, 30% in defensive assets like bonds and cash. It's the middle ground.

**[VISUAL: Growth option chart - 85-100% shares/property, high risk high return]**
**VO:** A Growth or High Growth option invests 85 to 100% in growth assets. Over the long term, it averages 8 to 10% returns. But it's volatile — you can lose 20% in a bad year.

**[VISUAL: Conservative option chart - 30-50% shares, mostly bonds/cash]**
**VO:** A Conservative option invests mostly in bonds and cash. Returns average 4 to 5%. Low risk, low reward. Suitable if you're already retired or close to it.

**[VISUAL: $100K Growth → $1M vs Balanced → $760K vs Conservative → $440K over 30 years]**
**VO:** Here's the real impact. $100,000 over 30 years: In Growth at 9% returns: roughly $1,000,000. In Balanced at 7%: roughly $760,000. In Conservative at 5%: roughly $430,000. The difference between Growth and Conservative? Over $500,000. For doing nothing except choosing a different option.

**[VISUAL: Age-based guide: 20-40 → Growth, 40-55 → Balanced/Growth, 55+ → Balanced, 65+ → Conservative]**
**VO:** Here's a simple age-based guide. Under 40: pick High Growth. You have 25+ years to ride out market volatility. 40 to 55: pick Balanced or Growth. 55 to 65: start shifting to Balanced. Retired or close: Conservative.

**[VISUAL: Phone screen showing how to change investment option in super fund app]**
**VO:** Changing is easy. Log into your super fund, go to Investment Options, and choose. It takes 2 minutes. Don't leave your retirement to default settings.
