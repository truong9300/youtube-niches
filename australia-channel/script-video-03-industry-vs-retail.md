### 00:30 – What's the Difference? (2:00)

**[VISUAL: Định nghĩa — Industry Fund = not-for-profit, Retail = for-profit]**

**VO:** The fundamental difference is simple. Industry Super Funds are run by unions and industry bodies. They're not-for-profit. Every dollar they earn goes back to members.

**[VISUAL: Logo AustralianSuper, Hostplus, HESTA, REST, Cbus, UniSuper]**

**VO:** The biggest names: AustralianSuper — the largest in Australia with over $340 billion. Hostplus for hospitality. HESTA for health. REST for retail. Cbus for construction. UniSuper for higher education.

**[VISUAL: Định nghĩa — Retail Fund = bank-owned, for-profit, shareholders]**

**VO:** Retail Super Funds are owned by banks and financial institutions. They're for-profit. They have shareholders to answer to. Names like AMP, MLC, Colonial First State, and Mercer.

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ market share — Industry ~40%, Retail ~30%, SMSF ~25%, Public ~5%]**

**VO:** About 40% of Australians are in Industry funds, 30% in Retail, 25% in Self-Managed, and the rest in public sector funds.

---

### 02:30 – The Fee Difference (2:00)

**[VISUAL: Kính lúp phóng to "Fees" trên tờ rơi super]**

**VO:** Here's where it gets interesting — fees.

**[VISUAL: Bảng so sánh phí — Industry: admin 0.5% + investment 0.4% = 0.9% total vs Retail: admin 0.7% + investment 0.6% = 1.3% total]**

**VO:** Industry funds typically charge total fees around 0.8% to 1.0% per year. Retail funds charge 1.1% to 1.5% or more.

**[VISUAL: "0.4% difference" — có vẻ nhỏ]**

**VO:** That 0.4% difference sounds tiny. But let me show you what it actually means.

**[VISUAL: Tính toán — $100K balance × 0.4% = $400/năm. Với $500K = $2,000/năm]**

**VO:** On a $100,000 balance, that's $400 a year. On $500,000, it's $2,000 a year.

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ compound — $400/năm × 30 năm × 7% returns]**

**VO:** If we assume 7% returns over 30 years, that $2,000 a year compounds to... $188,000.

**[VISUAL: CON SỐ — $188,000 — chữ "CHI PHÍ CỦA VIỆC CHỌN SAI QUỸ"]**

**VO:** $188,000. That's the cost of choosing a high-fee retail fund over a low-fee industry fund. For doing nothing differently except signing the wrong paperwork.

---

### 04:30 – Performance: Do Industry Funds Actually Perform Better? (2:00)

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ đường — 10-year returns Industry vs Retail]**

**VO:** But it's not just about fees. What about investment performance?

**[VISUAL: Dữ liệu — APRA 10-year median return: Industry 7.2% vs Retail 6.5%]**

**VO:** According to APRA data, over the past 10 years, Industry funds have consistently outperformed Retail funds. The median Industry fund returned about 7.2% annually. The median Retail fund returned 6.5%.

**[VISUAL: Chênh lệch 0.7%/năm × $100K × 30 năm]**

**VO:** That 0.7% performance gap, combined with lower fees, means the total advantage for Industry funds over 30 years is even bigger.

**[VISUAL: $100K → Industry: ~$761K vs Retail: ~$573K — chênh $188K]**

**VO:** $100,000 invested for 30 years: In an Industry fund at 7.2% returns with 0.9% fees → roughly $761,000. In a Retail fund at 6.5% returns with 1.3% fees → roughly $573,000. A difference of $188,000.

**[VISUAL: Chữ lớn: "$188,000"]**

**VO:** Same initial balance. Same time period. The only difference was choosing the right type of fund.

---

### 06:30 – When Retail Funds Make Sense (1:30)

**[VISUAL: Dấu "?" — liệu Retail có khi nào tốt hơn?]**

**VO:** So are Retail funds always the bad choice? Not necessarily.

**[VISUAL: Sản phẩm — financial advice, insurance, investment options]**

**VO:** Retail funds often offer more services — face-to-face financial advice, more insurance options, and a wider range of investment choices. If having access to an advisor is important to you, a Retail fund might be worth the extra cost.

**[VISUAL: Người tài chính cao — large balance, complex needs]**

**VO:** For high-net-worth individuals with complex financial situations, the advice and tailored products from a Retail fund could justify the higher fees.

**[VISUAL: Người trẻ, ít kinh nghiệm — balanced option là đủ]**

**VO:** But for the vast majority of Australians — especially young people — an Industry fund with a simple high-growth option is the better choice.

---

### 08:00 – How to Switch Your Super Fund (1:00)

**[VISUAL: myGov logo + ATO online services]**

**VO:** Switching your super fund is easier than most people think.

**[VISUAL: Step 1 — Log into myGov]**

**VO:** Step one: Log into myGov and link the ATO.

**[VISUAL: Step 2 — Find your super section]**

**VO:** Step two: Go to the Super section. It shows you ALL your super accounts — including ones you forgot about.

**[VISUAL: Step 3 — Choose a new fund and click]**

**VO:** Step three: Choose "Consolidate my super" and pick which fund you want to keep. The ATO handles the rest.

**[VISUAL: Step 4 — Check insurance]**

**VO:** Important: before consolidating, check what insurance you have in each fund. If you close a fund with life insurance you need, you'll lose it. Consider keeping a separate insurance-only account if necessary.

**[VISUAL: Thời gian — 10 phút là xong]**

**VO:** The whole process takes under 10 minutes. And it could save you $188,000.

**[VISUAL: Bảng tổng kết Industry vs Retail]**

**VO:** Here's the bottom line. For 90% of Australians, Industry Super Funds are the clear winner.
