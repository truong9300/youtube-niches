### 00:30 – How Super Gets Lost (1:30)

**[VISUAL: Timeline — changing jobs]**

**VO:** How does super get lost in the first place? It's surprisingly easy.

**[VISUAL: Người thay việc — job 1 → fund A, job 2 → fund B, job 3 → fund C]**

**VO:** Every time you change jobs, your new employer can put you into a different super fund. If you don't actively choose, they'll use their default fund. After 5 jobs, you could have 5 super accounts.

**[VISUAL: Người chuyển nhà, chuyển nước, quên cập nhật địa chỉ super]**

**VO:** Or you might have moved house, changed your name, or your employer entered your details wrong. The fund can't find you, so your account goes into "lost" status.

**[VISUAL: Icon small balances — dưới $6,000]**

**VO:** Accounts with less than $6,000 are especially vulnerable. The ATO automatically consolidates small inactive accounts into your active one — but only if they can match your details.

**[VISUAL: $17B — chia nhỏ: 6 triệu tài khoản lost]**

**VO:** Over 6 million lost super accounts exist in Australia. 6 million. That's almost one for every two employed Australians.

### 02:00 – Why You Should Care (1:00)

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ — fees đang ăn mòn balance]**

**VO:** Having multiple super accounts isn't just annoying — it's expensive.

**[VISUAL: 5 tài khoản × $80 fee/năm = $400/năm]**

**VO:** Each account charges fees. Administration fees, investment fees, insurance premiums. If you have 5 accounts with $2,000 each paying $80 in fees annually, that's $400 a year gone.

**[VISUAL: $400/năm × 30 năm × compound = $30,000+]**

**VO:** Over 30 years at 7% returns, that $400 a year compounds into over $30,000. Just in fees on money that's doing nothing for you.

**[VISUAL: Biểu tượng — lost super không được đầu tư]**

**VO:** And here's the worst part: lost super accounts are often invested in the default cash option, earning almost nothing. Your money is sitting there, being eaten by fees, growing at 1-2% instead of 7-8%.

### 03:00 – Step-by-Step: Find Your Lost Super (3:00)

**[VISUAL: myGov website → ATO → Super]**

**VO:** Here's how to find it. Step by step.

**[VISUAL: Step 1 — myGov login]**

**VO:** Step one: Go to my.gov.au and log in. If you don't have a myGov account, set one up — it takes 5 minutes and you need it for tax anyway.

**[VISUAL: Step 2 — Link ATO]**

**VO:** Step two: Link the Australian Tax Office to your myGov account. If you've done your tax return online, it's probably already linked.

**[VISUAL: Step 3 — Super section → "Manage my super"]**

**VO:** Step three: Go to the Super section and click "Manage my super". You'll see a page called "Your super accounts".

**[VISUAL: Screen shows — All your super accounts listed]**

**VO:** This page shows EVERY super account linked to your Tax File Number. Including ones you forgot existed.

**[VISUAL: Account with lost status + balance]**

**VO:** You'll see the fund name, member number, balance, and whether it's active, lost, or ATO-held.

**[VISUAL: Step 4 — Consolidate]**

**VO:** Step four: Click "Transfer super" and select the accounts you want to consolidate into your chosen fund.

**[VISUAL: Warning icon — insurance check]**

**VO:** Important warning: before consolidating, check what insurance each account has. If you have life insurance or income protection attached to a fund, closing it means losing that cover. Consider keeping a separate insurance-only account if needed.

**[VISUAL: Confirmation — done!]**

**VO:** That's it. The transfer takes 3-7 business days. Your lost super moves into your main account and starts earning real returns.

### 06:00 – What About ATO-Held Super? (1:30)

**[VISUAL: ATO icon + chữ "ATO-Held Super"]**

**VO:** If you have super that's been lost for more than 5 years, it gets transferred to the ATO.

**[VISUAL: ATO holding $17B in lost super]**

**VO:** The ATO currently holds over $17 billion in unclaimed super. And they're not investing it — it just sits there earning CPI interest, which is basically nothing.

**[VISUAL: myGov → ATO-held super → transfer]**

**VO:** To claim ATO-held super, log into myGov, go to Super → Manage → "Transfer to my super fund". Enter your fund details, and the ATO sends the money within 14 days.

**[VISUAL: Free search — ATO website, no login needed]**

**VO:** You can even search for lost super without logging in. Go to the ATO website and use the "SuperSeeker" tool. Just enter your TFN and date of birth.

### 08:30 – Prevent Future Lost Super (1:00)

**[VISUAL: Tip 1 — Choose one fund, stick with it]**

**VO:** Once you've consolidated, here's how to make sure this doesn't happen again.

**[VISUAL: Tip 1 icon: standard choice form]**

**VO:** One: When you start a new job, fill out the standard choice form and nominate your existing super fund. Your employer can't override your choice.

**[VISUAL: Tip 2: update details]**

**VO:** Two: Every time you move house or change your name, update your details with your super fund. It takes 2 minutes online.

**[VISUAL: Tip 3: check once a year]**

**VO:** Three: Once a year, log into myGov and check your super section for any new accounts you might have missed.

**[VISUAL: Tip 4: consolidate automatically]**

**VO:** Four: The ATO's automatic consolidation feature will merge small inactive accounts into your active one — but only if your details match across funds. So keep your name and address consistent.
