Imagine finding $10,000 in an old jacket pocket. That's basically what millions of Australians are doing right now — except the money is in a super account they forgot about.
According to the ATO, over $17 billion in lost and unclaimed super is sitting in inactive accounts. 17 billion dollars. Belonging to real people like you.
In this video, I'll show you exactly how to find your lost super, consolidate it, and put it to work — all in under 10 minutes.
How does super get lost in the first place? It's surprisingly easy.
Every time you change jobs, your new employer can put you into a different super fund. If you don't actively choose, they'll use their default fund. After 5 jobs, you could have 5 super accounts.
Or you might have moved house, changed your name, or your employer entered your details wrong. The fund can't find you, so your account goes into "lost" status.
Accounts with less than $6,000 are especially vulnerable. The ATO automatically consolidates small inactive accounts into your active one — but only if they can match your details.
Over 6 million lost super accounts exist in Australia. 6 million. That's almost one for every two employed Australians.
Having multiple super accounts isn't just annoying — it's expensive.
Each account charges fees. Administration fees, investment fees, insurance premiums. If you have 5 accounts with $2,000 each paying $80 in fees annually, that's $400 a year gone.
Over 30 years at 7% returns, that $400 a year compounds into over $30,000. Just in fees on money that's doing nothing for you.
And here's the worst part: lost super accounts are often invested in the default cash option, earning almost nothing. Your money is sitting there, being eaten by fees, growing at 1-2% instead of 7-8%.
Here's how to find it. Step by step.
Step one: Go to my.gov.au and log in. If you don't have a myGov account, set one up — it takes 5 minutes and you need it for tax anyway.
Step two: Link the Australian Tax Office to your myGov account. If you've done your tax return online, it's probably already linked.
Step three: Go to the Super section and click "Manage my super". You'll see a page called "Your super accounts".
This page shows EVERY super account linked to your Tax File Number. Including ones you forgot existed.
You'll see the fund name, member number, balance, and whether it's active, lost, or ATO-held.
Step four: Click "Transfer super" and select the accounts you want to consolidate into your chosen fund.
Important warning: before consolidating, check what insurance each account has. If you have life insurance or income protection attached to a fund, closing it means losing that cover. Consider keeping a separate insurance-only account if needed.
That's it. The transfer takes 3-7 business days. Your lost super moves into your main account and starts earning real returns.
If you have super that's been lost for more than 5 years, it gets transferred to the ATO.
The ATO currently holds over $17 billion in unclaimed super. And they're not investing it — it just sits there earning CPI interest, which is basically nothing.
To claim ATO-held super, log into myGov, go to Super → Manage → "Transfer to my super fund". Enter your fund details, and the ATO sends the money within 14 days.
You can even search for lost super without logging in. Go to the ATO website and use the "SuperSeeker" tool. Just enter your TFN and date of birth.
How much is typically found? The median lost super balance is about $3,500. But many people find much more.
I've seen people find $5,000, $10,000, even $50,000 in lost super. One woman in her 50s found $47,000 from a job she had 20 years ago.
If you find $10,000 and move it into a growth investment option, at 7% returns over 20 years, that becomes $38,700. For 10 minutes of work.
That's $232,000 an hour for your time. Best hourly rate you'll ever earn.
