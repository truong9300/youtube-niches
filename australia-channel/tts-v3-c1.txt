You're 35 years old and you have $100,000 in super. You have no idea which fund you're with — it's just whatever your employer chose.
Over the next 30 years, choosing the wrong type of super fund could cost you over $150,000. The right choice? It could make you hundreds of thousands richer.
In this video, we'll break down Industry Super Funds versus Retail Super Funds — the real difference in fees, returns, and what you should actually pick.
The fundamental difference is simple. Industry Super Funds are run by unions and industry bodies. They're not-for-profit. Every dollar they earn goes back to members.
The biggest names: AustralianSuper — the largest in Australia with over $340 billion. Hostplus for hospitality. HESTA for health. REST for retail. Cbus for construction. UniSuper for higher education.
Retail Super Funds are owned by banks and financial institutions. They're for-profit. They have shareholders to answer to. Names like AMP, MLC, Colonial First State, and Mercer.
About 40% of Australians are in Industry funds, 30% in Retail, 25% in Self-Managed, and the rest in public sector funds.
Here's where it gets interesting — fees.
Industry funds typically charge total fees around 0.8% to 1.0% per year. Retail funds charge 1.1% to 1.5% or more.
That 0.4% difference sounds tiny. But let me show you what it actually means.
On a $100,000 balance, that's $400 a year. On $500,000, it's $2,000 a year.
If we assume 7% returns over 30 years, that $2,000 a year compounds to... $188,000.
$188,000. That's the cost of choosing a high-fee retail fund over a low-fee industry fund. For doing nothing differently except signing the wrong paperwork.
But it's not just about fees. What about investment performance?
According to APRA data, over the past 10 years, Industry funds have consistently outperformed Retail funds. The median Industry fund returned about 7.2% annually. The median Retail fund returned 6.5%.
That 0.7% performance gap, combined with lower fees, means the total advantage for Industry funds over 30 years is even bigger.
$100,000 invested for 30 years: In an Industry fund at 7.2% returns with 0.9% fees → roughly $761,000. In a Retail fund at 6.5% returns with 1.3% fees → roughly $573,000. A difference of $188,000.
Same initial balance. Same time period. The only difference was choosing the right type of fund.
So are Retail funds always the bad choice? Not necessarily.
Retail funds often offer more services — face-to-face financial advice, more insurance options, and a wider range of investment choices. If having access to an advisor is important to you, a Retail fund might be worth the extra cost.
For high-net-worth individuals with complex financial situations, the advice and tailored products from a Retail fund could justify the higher fees.
But for the vast majority of Australians — especially young people — an Industry fund with a simple high-growth option is the better choice.
Switching your super fund is easier than most people think.
Step one: Log into myGov and link the ATO.
Step two: Go to the Super section. It shows you ALL your super accounts — including ones you forgot about.
Step three: Choose "Consolidate my super" and pick which fund you want to keep. The ATO handles the rest.
Important: before consolidating, check what insurance you have in each fund. If you close a fund with life insurance you need, you'll lose it. Consider keeping a separate insurance-only account if necessary.
The whole process takes under 10 minutes. And it could save you $188,000.
Here's the bottom line. For 90% of Australians, Industry Super Funds are the clear winner.
Do yourself a favour. Check what fund you're in. Compare fees. Compare performance. And if you're in a high-fee Retail fund, consider switching.
