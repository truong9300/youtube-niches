### 00:30 – What Is Superannuation? (1:30)

**[VISUAL: Lịch sử đồ hoạ — dòng thời gian từ 1992 đến 2026]**

**VO:** Let's start at the beginning. Superannuation isn't a choice. It's mandatory.

**[VISUAL: Toà nhà chính phủ + chữ "Keating 1992"]**

**VO:** Back in 1992, Prime Minister Paul Keating introduced the Superannuation Guarantee. The idea was simple — force employers to put money aside for workers' retirement, so people wouldn't rely solely on the Age Pension.

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ tăng dần 3% → 9% → 11.5%]**

**VO:** In 1992, it started at 3% of your salary. By 2002, it was 9%. Today, in 2026, it's 11.5% — and it's scheduled to rise to 12% by 2028.

**[VISUAL: Người lao động và ông chủ — minh hoạ employer contribution]**

**VO:** Here's what most people get wrong: this 11.5% is paid by YOUR EMPLOYER on top of your salary. It's not taken out of your take-home pay. It's extra money going into your super fund, invested in shares, property, and bonds, growing over decades.

### 03:30 – The Three Types of Super Funds (2:00)

**[VISUAL: 3 cột — Industry, Retail, Self-Managed]**

**VO:** In Australia, there are three main types of super funds.

**[VISUAL: Cột 1 — Industry Super Funds — AustralianSuper, Hostplus, HESTA]**

**VO:** First, Industry Super Funds. These are run by unions and industry bodies — not-for-profit. Names like AustralianSuper, Hostplus, and HESTA. They tend to have lower fees and historically better returns. Around 40% of Australians choose these.

**[VISUAL: Cột 2 — Retail Funds — AMP, MLC, Colonial First State]**

**VO:** Second, Retail Super Funds. These are run by banks and financial institutions — for-profit. AMP, MLC, Colonial First State. They charge higher fees, but offer more products and advice. About 30% of Australians use these.

**[VISUAL: Cột 3 — SMSF — người tự quản lý, biểu đồ chứng khoán]**

**VO:** Third, Self-Managed Super Funds — SMSFs. You run your own fund, choose your own investments. Only 5% of Australians do this — it's for people with at least $200,000 in super who want direct control.

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ so sánh phí — Industry 0.9%, Retail 1.3%, SMSF ~0.5%]**

**VO:** The difference in fees between an industry fund and a retail fund might not sound like much — 0.9% versus 1.3%. But on a $500,000 balance over 30 years, that 0.4% difference costs you over $150,000.

### 05:30 – Salary Sacrifice & Concessional Contributions (1:30)

**[VISUAL: Người đi làm văn phòng, mũi tên chỉ "Salary Sacrifice"]**

**VO:** Now let's talk about the most powerful tool in the Australian super system — salary sacrifice.

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ — $100 salary → either $63 take-home OR $85 into super]**

**VO:** Here's how it works. When you ask your employer to put extra pre-tax money into super, it's taxed at only 15% instead of your marginal tax rate — which could be 32.5%, 37%, or even 45%.

**[VISUAL: Tính toán — $10,000 sacrificed = $8,500 vào super vs $6,300 take-home]**

**VO:** The cap for these concessional contributions in 2026 is $30,000 per year, including your employer's 11.5%. So you can add roughly $18,500 extra yourself.

**[VISUAL: Người dùng app super, đồng hồ đếm ngược]**

**VO:** And if you didn't use your full cap in previous years, you can use unused caps going back 5 years. This is called the carry-forward rule, and it's massively underutilised.

### 07:00 – The Government's Super Co-Contribution (1:00)

**[VISUAL: Người làm part-time/casual — biểu tượng chính phủ]**

**VO:** Here's a free money hack that most low-income Australians don't know about.

**[VISUAL: Tiền từ chính phủ bay vào tài khoản super]**

**VO:** If you earn under $58,445 a year and make after-tax contributions to your super, the government will match 50 cents for every dollar you put in — up to $500 free.

**[VISUAL: Tính toán — bỏ $1,000 → nhận $500 free + investment returns]**

**VO:** So if you're a casual worker or part-time earner, putting just $1,000 into super after-tax gets you a free $500 from the government. That's an instant 50% return. No other investment in Australia guarantees that.

### 08:00 – When Can You Access Your Super? (1:00)

**[VISUAL: Dòng thời gian — 60 tuổi]**

**VO:** So when can you actually get this money?

**[VISUAL: Bảng — Preservation Age theo ngày sinh]**

**VO:** Your preservation age depends on when you were born. If you were born after 1964, your preservation age is 60. Once you turn preservation age AND retire, you can access your super.

**[VISUAL: 3 options — Lump Sum, Income Stream, Pension]**

**VO:** You can take it as a lump sum, set up an income stream, or move it into an account-based pension — which is tax-free once you're over 60.

**[VISUAL: Chữ "TAX-FREE" đập mạnh]**

**VO:** That's right. In Australia, once you turn 60, all super withdrawals are completely tax-free. It's one of the most generous retirement systems in the world.

**VO:** Before I wrap up, here are the most common mistakes Australians make:

**[VISUAL: Icon 1 — nhiều tài khoản super]**
