### 00:30 – The Old Rule of Thumb (1:00)

**[VISUAL: Biểu tượng "ASFA Retirement Standard"]**

**VO:** For years, the Association of Super Funds of Australia — ASFA — has published benchmarks.

**[VISUAL: ASFA numbers — $595K modest, $690K comfortable]**

**VO:** For a single person, ASFA says you need $595,000 for a modest retirement and $690,000 for a comfortable one. Couples need $690K and $790K respectively.

**[VISUAL: Dấu X đỏ — con số này đã lỗi thời]**

### 01:30 – The Real Target in 2026 (2:00)

**[VISUAL: Tính toán lại — ASFA cũ vs ASFA mới (2026 điều chỉnh)]**

**VO:** After adjusting for inflation and the cost-of-living crisis, here are the real numbers for 2026:

**[VISUAL: Bảng mới]**

**VO:** For a single person you need roughly $750,000 to $850,000 for a comfortable retirement. For a couple: $900,000 to $1,000,000.

| | Modest | Comfortable |
|-|:------:|:----------:|
| Single | $450K - $550K | $750K - $850K |
| Couple | $550K - $650K | $900K - $1M |

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ — how spending breaks down: housing, food, health, transport]**

**VO:** A comfortable retirement means: you own your home outright, you can afford private health insurance, a reasonable car, a domestic holiday once a year, and eating out occasionally. Not luxury — comfortable.

**[VISUAL: "Modest" — just the basics + a little extra]**

**VO:** Modest means: you own your home, you have basic health cover, limited travel, and you watch your spending.

### 03:30 – How to Calculate YOUR Number (2:00)

**[VISUAL: Công thức — 4% rule]**

**VO:** Financial planners use the 4% rule. The idea is simple: in retirement, you can safely withdraw 4% of your super balance each year without running out of money for 30 years.

**[VISUAL: Tính toán — $800K × 4% = $32,000/năm = $615/tuần]**

**VO:** So if you have $800,000, you can withdraw $32,000 per year. Combined with a part Age Pension, that's roughly $900 per week for a single person.

**[VISUAL: Máy tính — $50K/năm mong muốn → cần $1.25M]**

**VO:** If you want $50,000 a year in retirement income, you need $50,000 ÷ 4% = $1,250,000. That's your target number.

**[VISUAL: Công thức: Target = Annual Spend ÷ 0.04]**

**VO:** Here's a simple calculator. Take the annual income you want in retirement — say $45,000. Divide by 0.04. That gives you $1,125,000 — your super target.

**[VISUAL: Bảng nhanh]**

| Target income/year | Super balance needed |
|:-----------------:|:-------------------:|
| $30,000 | $750,000 |
| $40,000 | $1,000,000 |
| $50,000 | $1,250,000 |
| $60,000 | $1,500,000 |

### 05:30 – Where Does the Age Pension Fit? (1:30)

**[VISUAL: Centrelink logo + chữ "Age Pension"]**

**VO:** But here's what most people don't realise — the Age Pension exists. And for most Australians, it will be part of your retirement income.

**[VISUAL: Age Pension rates 2026 — single ~$1,100/fortnight, couple ~$1,660/fortnight]**

**VO:** In 2026, the full Age Pension is about $28,600 a year for a single person and $43,000 for a couple.

**[VISUAL: Means test — assets test + income test]**

**VO:** But the pension is means-tested. The more super and assets you have, the less pension you get.

**[VISUAL: Biểu đồ — assets test thresholds]**

**VO:** For a single homeowner: you start losing pension when your assets exceed $301,750. You lose it entirely at $674,000. For couples: $451,500 threshold, $1,012,500 cut-off.

**[VISUAL: "The Sweet Spot" — $400K-600K super + part pension]**

**VO:** The sweet spot for many Australians is having $400,000 to $600,000 in super plus a part Age Pension. This provides around $45,000-$55,000 a year in total income — very comfortable.
