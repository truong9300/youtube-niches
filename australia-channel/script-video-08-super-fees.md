# Super Fees Exposed: How 0.5% Could Cost You $150,000

**Độ dài:** ~8 phút | **Series:** Superannuation Explained (Video #8)

**[VISUAL: Magnifying glass over super statement, exposing hidden fees]**
**VO:** Your super fund charges fees. That's normal. But the amount you're paying — and more importantly, what you're getting for it — could be quietly costing you your retirement.

**[VISUAL: $500K balance, 0.5% fee difference = $2,500/year]**
**VO:** The average Australian super balance at retirement is about $500,000. If your fund charges 1.5% in fees instead of 1%, that's an extra $2,500 a year. Doesn't sound huge? Watch.

**[VISUAL: $2,500/year × 30 years × 7% compound = $250,000]**
**VO:** That $2,500 extra in fees, invested at 7% over 30 years compounds to over $250,000. You're paying a quarter of a million dollars in unnecessary fees. For what?

**[VISUAL: Fee types: Admin, Investment, Indirect Cost Ratio (ICR), Performance fees, Insurance premiums]**
**VO:** Here's what you're actually paying. Administration fees: usually $50 to $150 a year. Investment fees: a percentage of your balance. Indirect Cost Ratio (ICR): covers underlying fund costs. Performance fees: bonuses for fund managers. Insurance premiums: inside super.

**[VISUAL: BestSuper 0.8% vs AussieFund 1.5% comparison table]**
**VO:** Using the ATO's YourSuper comparison tool, you can see exactly which funds are underperforming. Funds that fail the annual performance test get flagged.

**[VISUAL: myGov YourSuper tool interface]**
**VO:** Go to myGov, link the ATO, and use the YourSuper comparison tool. It shows you every fund's fees and returns side by side. If your fund is in the bottom 10% two years in a row, it gets banned from accepting new members.

**[VISUAL: Tips - check ICR, avoid duplicated insurance, negotiate for large balances]**
**VO:** Three tips to cut fees. One: check your ICR — it's often hidden. Two: cancel duplicate insurance inside super. Three: if you have over $200K, some funds let you negotiate.
